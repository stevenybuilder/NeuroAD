========================================================================
  NeuroAD Discovery Engine — High-Level Pipeline Overview
  "Imaging finds it. Proteins confirm it. The system says what to test next."
========================================================================

The whole system is one idea: build ONE probe on a frozen brain-MRI
foundation model, ask it THREE different questions, and treat the
DISAGREEMENT between the answers as the actual discovery. Everything
downstream is a filter that only lets real biology through.


========================================================================
  SEND-TO-A-TEAMMATE SUMMARY (prose version)
========================================================================

HOW THE PIPELINE WORKS, FROM A DATA-SCIENCE ANGLE
The core trick is that we build exactly ONE probe on top of a frozen
brain-MRI foundation model (Neuro-JEPA, pretrained on 1.5M+ scans) and
never retrain that encoder. Every scan just gets turned into a latent
structural embedding — no labels, no fine-tuning, which is why it's
cheap and generalizes. Then we take a single lightweight attentive-MLP
probe and point it at three different label columns: disease
(AD-vs-CN / conversion), scanner/site ID, and a molecular biomarker
(p-tau217 / GFAP). On top of that we run unsupervised clustering
(k-means / HDBSCAN) directly on the raw embedding vectors to surface
patient subgroups nobody labeled. The actual product isn't any single
prediction — it's the DISAGREEMENT between those answers. A signal that
predicts disease but also predicts the scanner is suspect; a signal that
predicts disease AND p-tau217 is real biology. Interpreting that
disagreement is where the human science happens.

THE "KILL WEAK, SURFACE STRONG" REFEREE
Every candidate signal has to survive a falsification gauntlet before
it's allowed to mean anything biological: age/sex adjustment, site/
scanner leakage checks, brain-age and atrophy controls, a held-out
replication split, and a biomarker anchor. We grade each survivor on a
verdict band — fragile -> partially robust -> robust enough for
follow-up -> strong candidate — and only "partially robust" and up get
promoted. This is the part that keeps us from burning months of real lab
time chasing an artifact, which is the classic failure mode in this
field.

WHAT WE DO WITH THE BIOMARKERS
An MRI signal can't validate itself, so the biomarker bridge asks
whether a SURVIVING imaging signal lines up with an independent
molecular axis — and, crucially, WHICH biomarker it tracks routes the
mechanism. If it tracks plasma p-tau217, we route it to amyloid/tau
biology (that's our primary anchor, and we kill the claim if the
correlation r < 0.2). If it tracks GFAP more, we route to glial/
inflammatory biology. If it tracks NfL or white-matter-hyperintensity
burden instead, we call it generic vascular/axonal injury, not
Alzheimer's-specific. If it tracks nothing molecular, we honestly label
it "unanchored, imaging-only"; if it fails the scanner/age checks, we
refuse it as an artifact. Amyloid status is used to ENRICH the tau
route, never as the standalone proof. So the biomarkers convert a raw
embedding direction into a controlled, falsifiable hypothesis with a
named mechanism.

HOW RANKED CANDIDATE PROTEIN DRUG DISCOVERY WORKS
Only signals that survive the referee AND get a molecular route reach
the discovery layer. There we bake in PI4AD (open-source) logic:
multi-omic scoring over GWAS + QTL/PCHi-C + STRING protein networks,
random-walk-with-restart network propagation, self-organizing-map
clustering to separate AD-specific biology from other disease, and PCST
pathway-crosstalk analysis to find nodal hub genes (e.g. Ras-signaling
hubs like HRAS/MAPK1), plus drug-repurposing enrichment. That produces
candidate proteins/genes RANKED 0-10 by priority, and we sanity-check
the ranking by confirming it recovers already-validated targets like APP
and ESR1 — so it's calibrated, not arbitrary. The top-ranked candidates
then go through AlphaFold 3 / Boltz structural modeling to ground
compound and repurposing decisions in real 3D interactions. The final
output for each survivor is deliberately NOT a dashboard: it's one
biomarker-routed mechanism hypothesis -> one ranked protein target ->
one falsifiable wet-lab experiment in a fast human model (iPSC or 3D
"Alzheimer's-in-a-dish" organoids, ~6 weeks instead of decades), with
every mechanistic sentence paired to the artifact and protein evidence
behind it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Neuro-JEPA, pre-trained on 1.5M+ brain scans, is FROZEN. We never
  retrain it. Each scan becomes a latent structural embedding vector.
  No labels needed to produce these. This is why the method is cheap
  and generalizes: no new training data, no fine-tuning.

                              │
                              ▼
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STAGE 2 — ONE PROBE, THREE QUESTIONS   (the science)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  A single lightweight attentive-MLP probe sits on the frozen embedding
  (+ FastSurfer hippocampal/cortical volumes for interpretable grounding).
  We point the SAME head at different label columns:

    Q1  Disease   -> conversion / AD-vs-CN   "on a trajectory to AD?"
    Q2  Scanner   -> site / scanner ID        "is this just the machine?"
    Q3  Protein   -> p-tau217 / GFAP          "tied to molecular pathology?"

                     "what subgroups exist that NOBODY labeled?"

  between the three answers — a signal that predicts disease but also

     tracks p-tau217   -> amyloid / tau route      (primary anchor)
     tracks GFAP       -> glial inflammation        (secondary anchor)

========================================================================
  ADDITIONAL TECHNICAL DEPTH
  (the models under each stage — what's frozen, what we train,
   what's roadmap. The diagram above is the flow; this is the how.)
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  A. THE FOUNDATION ENCODER (Stage 1) — Neuro-JEPA, FROZEN
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Architecture: a V-JEPA-2-style 3D vision transformer with a
    Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) backbone. It is a transformer, but a
    self-supervised one that predicts masked latent structure rather
    than reconstructing pixels (JEPA = Joint-Embedding Predictive
    Architecture: predict in EMBEDDING space, not voxel space).
  - Pretraining: ~1.55M curated structural scans (T1w/T2w/FLAIR),
    self-supervised, no disease labels. "Multimodal" here = multiple
    structural MRI sequences fused, NOT structural + functional.
  - We DO NOT fine-tune it. Run once, cache one fixed-dimension
    embedding vector per subject, then probe the table. Two reasons:
      (1) Technical — frozen self-supervised features generalize and
          need no training data; the repo ships "attentive probing"
          (frozen backbone + trainable head) natively.
      (2) Licensing — weights are CC BY-NC-ND (NoDerivatives); a
          fine-tuned checkpoint is a derivative and off the table.
          Frozen-encoder + our-own-head stays clean.
  - Built against an EMBEDDING INTERFACE, not Neuro-JEPA specifically:
    downstream only expects one fixed-dim vector per subject, so the
    feeder is swappable (real Neuro-JEPA embeddings / a substitute open
    encoder / weight-free structural features like hippocampal volume,
    cortical thickness, WMH burden).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  B. THE PROBE / "MLP" (Stage 2) — the ONLY thing we train
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Deliberately tiny. Start with a LINEAR probe (logistic regression
    on the frozen embedding); reach for a small 2-layer MLP only if the
    linear probe leaves obvious signal on the table.
  - Why start linear: a linear probe that predicts the outcome is a
    STRONGER scientific claim than an MLP that does — it proves the
    signal is cleanly, linearly present in the embedding, not something
    the head had to manufacture. It also keeps "is the signal the
    encoder's or ours?" unambiguous.
  - The "attentive MLP probe" adds a lightweight attention pool over the
    encoder's patch/token features (frozen backbone -> trainable head),
    plus leave-one-group-out attribution so we can say WHAT drives the
    signal (embedding vs. plasma). Exact hippocampal/cortical volumes
    come from FastSurfer, not learned — that's the interpretable
    grounding.
  - Key architectural insight: ONE head, swap the label column ->
    three of the four tools. Same code predicts disease, scanner, and
    p-tau217. The disagreement between those three fits IS the product.
    (Optional Cox/survival head answers "how fast does it convert?" —
    a native Neuro-JEPA time-to-event task, nice-to-have not core.)
  - Explicitly NOT used: adapters / LoRA / unfreezing. They re-enter the
    NoDerivatives license and buy us nothing here.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  C. THE "MULTIMODAL TRANSFORMER FUSION LAYER" (Stage 3 in pipeline.txt)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Honest scoping note for the team:
  - In pipeline.txt this box reads "Cross-Attention & Graph Networks."
    That is the ROADMAP / full-vision form of the fusion step.
  - The shipped MVP does the fusion the cheap, defensible way: the
    frozen embedding is concatenated with tabular clinical features
    (cognitive tests, plasma p-tau217, demographics, FastSurfer volumes)
    and fed to the same small probe. That IS the cross-modal fusion for
    the demo — no separate transformer required.
  - A learned cross-attention / graph-network fusion (and a functional
    backbone, Brain-JEPA, for fMRI) is a Phase-1+ upgrade, not a demo
    dependency. When the functional track lands, Neuro-JEPA flips role
    to being the STRUCTURAL CONTROL in the referee gauntlet.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  D. CLUSTERING (Stage 2, the Detective) — unsupervised, no head
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - k-means / HDBSCAN run directly on the raw embedding vectors (or on a
    UMAP projection for inspection). No label, no training target.
  - This is the genuine discovery step: it surfaces subgroups nobody
    labeled. Every supervised probe (disease / scanner / biomarker) then
    re-runs PER CLUSTER, which is how "this subgroup converts faster and
    carries a tau signature but is NOT a scanner artifact" falls out.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  E. THE REFEREE — the statistics, not a model (Stage 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Each test is a cheap re-fit, not a new network:
    Age/sex adjustment  -> re-fit the probe with covariates
    Site/scanner leakage-> same head, label = scanner (the star test)
    Brain-age control   -> control for embedding-derived brain age
                           (native Neuro-JEPA strength, R^2 ~= 0.89)
    Biomarker anchor    -> correlate the phenotype with p-tau217 / GFAP
    Replication split   -> refit on a held-out site / cohort
  Verdict bands: fragile -> partially robust -> robust enough for
  follow-up -> strong candidate. Only "partially robust" and up pass.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  F. THE OMICS / STRUCTURE STACK (Stages 5-6) — mostly configured, not trained
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - PI4AD is an existing open-source R framework we bake in as logic, not
    a model we train: multi-omic meta-analysis scoring, Random-Walk-with-
    Restart (RWR) propagation over STRING networks, ANN self-organizing
    maps for AD-specific clustering, and Prize-Collecting Steiner Tree
    (PCST) for pathway crosstalk -> nodal hub genes. Output: genes ranked
    0-10. Calibration check: it recovers known targets (APP, ESR1).
  - Optional repurposing layer: graph models (TxGNN / CompGCN-style GNNs
    on biomedical knowledge graphs like PrimeKG) + LLM evidence synthesis
    for ranking known compounds.
  - Structure layer: AlphaFold 3 / Boltz inference (frozen, API or local)
    on the top-ranked candidates only — 3D interaction modeling to ground
    compound/repurposing decisions. Not trained by us.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  G. THE ORCHESTRATION LAYER — Claude as the harness
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Claude ties the stages together via tool-calling: it invokes the
    Neuro-JEPA encoder, the probe/referee code, the biomarker bridge,
    PI4AD logic, AlphaFold, and literature/RAG search, then synthesizes
    the auditable output.
  - Design contract that makes the whole thing swappable: a single
    cached-embedding table is the interface every downstream piece reads
      subject_id, embedding[d], dx, conversion, age, sex, site,
      scanner, amyloid, p_tau217, gfap, nfl, apoe
    The moment that schema exists, downstream is data-source-independent
    — swapping real embeddings in later changes exactly one file.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  TL;DR for an engineer
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  One frozen 3D transformer (Neuro-JEPA, MoE, we never touch its
  weights) -> one tiny linear/MLP probe we DO train, pointed at three
  labels -> unsupervised clustering to find subgroups -> a battery of
  cheap statistical re-fits (the referee) -> correlate survivors to
  biomarkers -> hand survivors to off-the-shelf omics (PI4AD) and
  structure (AlphaFold) tools -> Claude orchestrates and writes the
  auditable experiment card. The heavy models are all frozen/external;
  the only thing we train is the small probe, and that's on purpose.
========================================================================
