# Imaging's complementary value — conversion prediction where plasma is silent

MCI→AD conversion from structural imaging alone (ComBat-harmonized morphometry),
scored through the leakage-honest probe (site-disjoint CV, in-fold PCA, bootstrap
CI, permutation null). Generated by `scripts/run_conversion_imaging_only.py` →
`reports/conversion_imaging_only.json`.

**The setup.** The fusion analysis scores every modality on the same people, so it
keeps only subjects with a plasma draw: **498 subjects / 142 converters**. That
discards **701 conversion-labeled subjects (270 converters)** whose only omission
is a blood test. Imaging can serve them; plasma cannot.

## Results

| Slice | n (converters) | Imaging-only AUC [95% CI] | p (perm) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plasma-present | 498 (142) | 0.728 [0.679, 0.773] | 0.001 |
| **Plasma-absent** | **701 (270)** | **0.683 [0.645, 0.722]** | **0.001** |
| Full cohort | 1,199 (412) | 0.650 [0.616, 0.684] | 0.001 |

Plasma p-tau217 alone predicts conversion at AUC 0.814 — on the people who *have* a
draw. For the 701 who don't, that number is unavailable; imaging delivers a real,
CI-clears-chance **0.68** where the blood test offers nothing.

## Why this matters

- **Complementary, not competing.** Imaging doesn't beat plasma on the plasma-positive
  population (it shouldn't — plasma p-tau217 is excellent there). It covers the
  population plasma leaves behind: no blood draw, no assay access, or a test not yet
  ordered.
- **The converters were already here.** Extending to the imaging-only arm nearly
  **triples** the usable converter count (142 → 412) with zero new data acquisition —
  the 270 extra converters were being dropped solely for lacking plasma.
- **Honest scope.** Structural imaging is a weaker prognostic substrate than plasma;
  this is a reach-and-coverage result (who can be served), not a peak-accuracy claim.
