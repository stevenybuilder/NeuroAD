# Does imaging catch converters the blood test reassures?

Imaging-only MCI→AD conversion prediction *within biomarker-negative subgroups*,
scored through the leakage-honest probe (site-disjoint CV, in-fold PCA, bootstrap
CI, permutation null). Generated by `scripts/run_conversion_biomarker_negative.py`
→ `reports/conversion_biomarker_negative.json`.

Cohort: ADNI conversion-labeled **and** plasma-tested (n=498, 142 converters).
p-tau217 split at the median of the triangulated z-scored ensemble.

## Results

| Subgroup | n (converters) | Imaging-only AUC | Plasma-only AUC |
|---|---|---|---|
| **p-tau217 LOW** (blood test low) | 248 (23) | **0.770 [0.68, 0.85]** | 0.644 [0.51, 0.76] |
| p-tau217 HIGH | 250 (119) | 0.636 [0.56, 0.71] | 0.694 [0.63, 0.76] |
| Amyloid-negative | 424 (57) | 0.661 [0.58, 0.74] | 0.630 [0.55, 0.71] |
| Amyloid-positive | 464 (223) | 0.642 [0.59, 0.69] | 0.635 [0.58, 0.68] |

## Reading

- **The headline:** among people the blood test calls **low-risk** (p-tau217 below
  median), imaging predicts conversion at **AUC 0.77** while plasma collapses toward
  chance (0.64, CI nearly touches 0.5). Imaging flags converters the biomarker
  would reassure. Same pattern, weaker, in the amyloid-negative group (imaging 0.66,
  edging plasma 0.63).
- **The honest contrast:** among p-tau217-HIGH people, **plasma wins** (0.69 vs
  imaging 0.64) — plasma's wheelhouse. This is *complementarity*, not "imaging beats
  plasma": each modality carries the signal where the other goes quiet.
- **Caveat:** the low-p-tau subgroup has only 23 converters, so the CI is wide
  (though it clears chance). Treat as a strong directional finding, not a locked
  point estimate.
